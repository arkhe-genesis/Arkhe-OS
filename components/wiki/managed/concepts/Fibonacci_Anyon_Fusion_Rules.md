# [[Fibonacci Anyon Fusion Rules]]

## Summary
> Non‑Abelian anyon statistics where two τ anyons fuse to either vacuum (1) or a τ anyon with probabilities φ⁻² and φ⁻¹ respectively, where φ is the golden ratio.

## Detailed Description
Fibonacci anyons are a type of non‑Abelian anyon that can be used for topologically protected quantum computation. Their fusion rules are:
- τ × τ = 1 + τ
- τ × 1 = τ
- 1 × 1 = 1

The probability of vacuum outcome after a single braid is φ⁻² = ( (1+√5)/2 )⁻² ≈ 0.381966. This is the **Gold Standard** for the BIP‑1 hardware.
