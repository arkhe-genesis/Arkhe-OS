# [[The Quantum Oasis Effect]]

## Summary
> Hypothesis that the microtubule lattice provides a protective environment that extends quantum coherence times beyond what would be expected in bulk aqueous solution, possibly due to ordered water, dielectric screening, or topological shielding.

## Detailed Description
The term “quantum oasis” was introduced by Berg et al. (2026) to explain why their measured coherence time T₂ = 120 µs in microtubules was an order of magnitude longer than theoretical predictions (Jones et al. 2024 predicted <10 µs). The effect may arise from:
- The hydrophobic interior of the microtubule lumen.
- Collective phonon modes that decouple environmental noise.
- The intrinsic topology of the tubulin lattice acting as a passive error‑correcting code.

## Experimental Evidence
- [[2026-04-04_anyons_in_microtubules]] – T₂ = 120 µs at room temperature, contradicting earlier predictions.

## Contradictions
- **Jones et al. (2024)** – predicted T₂ < 10 µs. The discrepancy suggests the need for a revised model of decoherence in cytoskeletal structures.
